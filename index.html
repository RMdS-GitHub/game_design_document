Concept statement

The game in a tweet: one or two sentences at most that say what the game is and why it’s fun.

A game concept statement or premise is a brief, direct description of the situation of a game. It describes the goal of the player, the resistance to this goal and the means by which this goal is achieved. If it's all about the narrative part of the script, the game concept reads like a script. Realistically, the gameplay is described as it influences some elements of storytelling. A short example could be as follows:

"In Trick or Treat, players are trapped in the labyrinth of an old haunted house, escaping by destroying enemy monsters, dodging traps, and solving the maze." Trick or Treat is a third-person action-spying game. "

The purpose of this letter is to give the reader a sense of the game. It should answer these basic questions:

  • What is the goal of the game?
  • How is the goal of the game achieved?
  • What are the challenges to the game?
  • Where does the game take place?

Most people want to add marketing jargon and implementation specifications. That's just a mistake. Reporting that it's the best game ever, or that it will be available for the PS10 in 2020, simply does not strengthen a concept. To put it bluntly: This statement is about the concept. Concepts are general terms at a high level. For example, the concept of a car on the Indianapolis 500 did not begin with the use of carbon fiber.

Do not include the following elements in your letter:

  • Gaming platform (eg for the NES, because I'm retro-chic)
  • Game rating (eg "M" for adults)
  • Special features of the game (eg controls)
  • Game programming details (using, for example, recursive algorithms for speed)
  • Marketing (eg "more exciting than a rollercoaster ride")

There are exceptions to every rule. Sometimes it is important to add implementation details. These are exceptionally rare situations, eg. For example, a game designed solely for use with a new controller type or for use on a non-standard gaming platform. In these cases, it makes sense to touch the distinctive detail.

Genre(s)

Single genre is clearer but often less interesting. Genre combinations can be risky. Beware of ‘tired’ genres.

The following is a list of all commonly defined video game genres.

  • Action:
    Platform games, Shooter games, Fighting games, Beat 'em up games, Stealth game, Survival games, Battle Royale games, Rhythm games.
  • Action-adventure:
    Survival horror, Metroidvania.
  • Adenture:
    Text adventures, Graphic adventures, Visual novels, Interactive movie, Real-time 3D adventures.
  • Role-playing:
    Action RPG, MMORPG, Roguelikes, Tactical RPG, Sandbox RPG, JRPG, Monster Collection, First-person party-based RPG.
